# Replit Agent Prompt — Deeper Intake & Brief Generation

Paste this entire prompt into the Replit agent chat.

---

Add a "deeper intake" flow and brief generation feature to the existing app. This builds on the current 5-question assessment and results page.

## Overview of what to build

1. **CTA on the results page** — a prompt at the bottom inviting users to answer more questions and get connected with a professional
2. **Deeper intake flow** — 12 additional multiple-choice questions across 6 short modules
3. **Brief generation** — a structured professional summary built from the user's answers
4. **Brief review screen** — user sees the brief before sending, with a "Connect Me with a Professional" button (placeholder for now — just show a confirmation message)

---

## Part 1: Add CTA to the results page

At the bottom of the results page, below the "Experts Who Can Help" section, add a new section with a distinct background (light warm tone, slightly set apart from the rest of the page):

**Headline:** "Want Personalized Help?"

**Body text:** "Answer a few more questions and we'll prepare a brief summary of your situation — so a professional already understands your case before you ever speak to them. No names required. Takes about 3 minutes."

**Button:** "Get Matched with a Professional →" (gold/amber, same style as existing CTAs)

Clicking this button opens the deeper intake flow (new page at `/intake` or as a continuation of the existing `/start` flow — your choice based on what's cleaner in the codebase).

---

The intake should feel like a natural extension of the assessment — same design language, same progress bar, same card layout. Each module is a brief pause with a short label ("About the person", "Financial picture", etc.) displayed above the question.

All questions are multiple choice. No free-text fields.

**Q1:** What state are you in?

## Part 3: Brief generation

Generate the brief entirely from the intake answers — no external API call needed. Use the answers to fill a structured template with the following sections. Store the intake answers in component state and pass them to a `generateBrief()` function that returns the brief as a structured object.

### Brief structure

**Section 1 — Situation Overview**
A 2–3 sentence plain-language summary synthesizing the scenario. Use the situation type from Q1 of the original assessment (already in state) combined with the intake answers.

Examples:
- "An adult child is managing the estate of a recently deceased parent in [State]. A will is in place. The estate includes real estate and retirement accounts, with a total estimated value in the $500K–$1M range."
- "A family is seeking elder law guidance for a parent, currently in a nursing home in [State], with a dementia diagnosis. No Power of Attorney or Healthcare Proxy is currently in place."

**Section 2 — Key Facts**
Display as a clean two-column grid of label/value pairs:
- State: [Q1]
- Age range: [Q2]
- Current setting: [Q3]
- Cognitive decline: [Q4]
- Estimated assets: [Q5]
- Real estate involved: [Q6]
- Retirement/business interests: [Q7]
- Urgency: [from original assessment Q3]

**Section 3 — Legal Documents**
Two sub-lists side by side:
- In place: [list based on Q8, Q9, and original assessment Q4]
- Missing or unknown: [remainder]

**Section 4 — Family Context**
Short 1–2 sentence summary using Q10 and Q11:
- "Surviving spouse is present. No known family disputes."
- "No surviving spouse. Potential family disagreements noted — professional should be aware."

**Section 5 — What's Needed**
1–2 sentences derived from the situation type and the original assessment Q5 (concerns):
- "Family is seeking guidance on probate process and asset distribution."
- "Family needs Medicaid planning support and help establishing legal documents urgently."

**Section 6 — What's Already Been Done**
One sentence from Q12:
- "An attorney has already been consulted."
- "No attorney has been contacted yet."

## Part 4: Brief review screen

Show the generated brief on a clean review screen at `/brief` (or equivalent).

**Page headline:** "Your Situation Summary"

**Subhead:** "This is what a professional will see before speaking with you. No personal identifying information is included."

Display all 6 sections of the brief in a clean, readable layout — use cards or bordered sections to separate each one.

Below the brief, add two elements:

1. A reassurance note in smaller text: *"Your name, address, and contact information are not included in this summary. You control what gets shared and when."*

2. A large CTA button: **"Connect Me with a Professional →"** (gold/amber)

For now, clicking the button should show a simple confirmation message on the same page:
> "Thank you — your brief has been submitted. A professional in your area will review it and reach out if they're a good fit. This feature is coming soon — check back shortly."

---

## Design guidance

- Match the existing site design throughout — same colors, fonts, card styles
- Module labels (e.g. "About the person") should appear as a small, muted label above each question card, so users understand there's structure to the flow
- Progress bar should continue from the assessment (or restart at 1 of 12 for the intake — either is fine)
- The brief review screen should feel polished and trustworthy — this is what convinces the user the platform knows what it's doing
- Tone of all generated text: calm, professional, plain English — like a knowledgeable friend summarizing a situation to a colleague

After building, deploy the updated app.
